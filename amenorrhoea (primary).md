# Clinical features
Primary amenorrhoea is failure to establish menstruation by: 
- Age 16 years in girls with normal secondary characteristics -> Refer to gynae
- Age 14 years in girls with no secondary sexual characteristics -> Refer to paeds

Associated complications:
- [[osteoporosis]], CVD, infertility, Psychological distress
# Assessment
- Exclude pregnancy
- Previous chemo/radiotherapy
- cyclical abdo pain w/o bleeding
- assess development of secondary sexual characteristics
- Medication
- Illicit drug use
- Check Gonadotropins:
	- Low levels indicate a hypothalamic cause of amenorrhoea, whereas raised levels suggest an ovarian problem (e.g. Premature ovarian failure)
	- Raised: Gonadal dysgenesis (e.g. Turner's syndrome)
- Check Androgens: raised in PCOS
- Check Prolactin, Oestradiol.
