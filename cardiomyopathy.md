In cardiomyopathy, the muscle structure changes, so the heart may:
- pump weakly
- become stiff and unable to fill
- develop abnormal rhythms

This can eventually lead to [[Heart Failure]], arrhythmias, or sudden cardiac death in severe cases.

# Types
1. dilated- MC, due to viral myocarditis, alcohol, pregnancy, chemotherapy
2. hypertrophic- genetic, thickness especially around the septum. Sudden death in young athletes
3. restrictive- Stiff. Poor filling. Due to infiltration diseases like amyloidosis, [[sarcoidosis]], [[haemochromatosis]].
4. Arrhythmogenic right ventricular cardiomyopathy (ARVC)- Fat and scar replace the right ventricular muscle. genetic. death in young athletes
