# No Antibiotics
A no antibiotic prescribing or delayed antibiotic prescribing approach is generally recommended for patients with acute [[otitis]] media, acute sore throat/acute pharyngitis/acute tonsillitis, common cold, acute [[rhinosinusitis]] or acute cough/acute bronchitis.

# Antibiotics
See [[otitis|otitis media]] for when to give antibiotics. 
See [[tonsillitis]] for CENTOR criteria- >=3 criteria. 

## When at risk of developing complications
Give immediate antibiotics when
- systemically unwell
- serious illness: pneumonia, mastoiditis, peritonsillar abscess, peritonsillar cellulitis, intraorbital/intracranial complications 
- risk of complication due to pre-existing comorbidity: heart/lung/liver/neuromuscular/immunosuppression/[[cystic fibrosis|CF]]/young children who were born prematurely. 
- Age > 65yo + acute cough + >=2 of OR Age >80yo + acute cough + >=1 of
	- hospitalisation in previous year
	- type 1 or type 2 diabetes
	- history of congestive heart failure
	- current use of oral glucocorticoids

### Guidelines also recommend to specify how long the infections may last:
- Acute otitis media: 4 days
- Acute sore throat/acute pharyngitis/acute tonsillitis: 1 week
- common cold: 1 1/2 weeks
- acute rhinosinusitis: 2 1/2 weeks
- acute cough/acute bronchitis: 3 weeks
