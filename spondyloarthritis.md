Spondyloarthritis (SpA) symptom can be musculoskeletal, involving enthesitis. 
# Classification of arthritis
Inflammatory: [[rheumatoid arthritis]], psoriatic arthritis, [[ankylosing spondylitis]], [[reactive arthritis]], [[gout]] 
Non-inflammatory: [[ra vs osteoarthritis|Osteoarthritis]]

Seropositive (RF or Anti-CCP +ve): RA
[[seronegative spondyloarthropathy|seronegative arthritis]]: Typically part of [[spondyloarthritis]] family.

Spondyloarthritis: This is a family of inflammatory arthritides.
e.g. Ankylosing spondylitis, Psoriatic arthritis, Reactive arthritis, Enteropathic arthritis (IBD-related)

Axial vs Peripheral Spondyloarthritis
Modern classification (ASAS criteria) divides SpA into:
Axial spondyloarthritis (axSpA)

Primarily spine and sacroiliac joints.

Two subtypes:

Radiographic axSpA

Visible sacroiliitis on X-ray
= classic Ankylosing spondylitis

Non-radiographic axSpA

MRI inflammation but normal X-ray

Peripheral spondyloarthritis

Mainly:

Asymmetric oligoarthritis

Enthesitis

Dactylitis

Seen in:

Psoriatic arthritis

Reactive arthritis

IBD-associated arthritis
# Clinical Features
**Enthesitis**: Inflammation of tendonous or ligamentous insertions onto bone, is one of the most characteristic findings of spondyloarthritis. 
The most common sites of inflammation include the 
- Achilles tendon and 
- the plantar fascial insertions, although 
- patella tendonitis can also occur. 

Extra-articular manifestations: 
- [[psoriasis]], involvement of the ligamentous and tendinous insertions onto the pelvic bones

**Dactylitis** or 'sausage digits':
- Fusiform swelling of the entire digit due to inflammation and swelling of the flexor tenosynynovium, is another typical finding of the SpA.
### Missed picture %%linked%%
>[!warning]
>Axial presentations of spondyloarthritis are often misdiagnosed as mechanical low back pain, leading to delays in access to effective treatments. Peripheral presentations are often seen as unrelated joint or tendon problems, and can be misdiagnosed because problems can move around between joints

# Inflammatory arthritis (Not seronegative arthritis)
 - Crystal induced arthritis: [[gout]], [[pseudogout]] (Inflammatory but not autoimmune)
 - Connective tissue disease related: [[systemic lupus erythematosis|SLE]] (Photosensitivity), [[sjogren's syndrome|Sjögren Syndrome]], [[systemic sclerosis]], Mixed connective tissue disorder
 - Infective: [[septic arthritis]], post-viral (e.g. parvovirus)
