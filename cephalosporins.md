Broad-spectrum β-lactam antibiotics.

# Pharmacology
Mechanism similar to Penicillins
Excretion: mainly renal
CSF penetration:
- Poor unless meninges are inflamed
- Good CNS options: Cefotaxime, Ceftriaxone

### Penicillin Cross-Reactivity
1st & early 2nd generation: up to 10%
3rd generation: about 2–3%

# Cephalosporin Generations

| Cephalosporin Class / Generation | Drugs                                          |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| First generation (oral)          | Cefalexin, Cefradine, Cefadroxil               |
| Second generation                | Cefaclor, Cefuroxime, Cefuroxime axetil        |
| Third generation                 | Cefixime, Cefotaxime, Ceftazidime, Ceftriaxone |
| Fifth generation                 | Ceftaroline fosamil                            |
| Siderophore cephalosporin        | Cefiderocol                                    |
## First Generation (oral)
**Drugs**: Cefalexin, Cefradine, Cefadroxil
**Spectrum / Uses**: UTI, RTI, Otitis media, Skin and soft-tissue infections
### Notes
Cefadroxil: Long duration of action, BD dosing
Poor activity vs H. influenzae

---
## Second Generation
*2nd and 3rd gen are high risk of [[c. difficile]].* 
### Cefaclor (oral)
- Similar antimicrobial spectrum to first generation
- Good activity against H. influenzae
### Cefuroxime
- Less susceptible to β-lactamase inactivation
- Greater activity against H. influenzae
- Active against bacteria resistant to earlier cephalosporins
### Cefuroxime axetil 
oral ester/prodrug of cefuroxime
- Poor absorption
- Must be taken with food

---
## Third Generation
### General Features
- Greater activity against Gram-negative bacteria
- Less active vs Gram-positive bacteria compared with cefuroxime (notably Staphylococcus aureus)
- Broad spectrum → risk of superinfection (resistant bacteria/fungi)
### Cefixime (oral)
- Longer duration of action than other oral cephalosporins
- Licensed only for acute infections
### Cefotaxime
- Good CNS penetration
- Used in meningitis
### Ceftazidime
- Strong activity against Pseudomonas
- Active against other Gram-negative bacteria
### Ceftriaxone
- Long half-life → once-daily dosing
#### Indications
- Septicaemia
- Pneumonia
- Meningitis
#### Important Side Effect
- Biliary precipitation
- Calcium salt may precipitate in gallbladder
- Usually resolves after stopping drug

---
## Fifth Generation
### Ceftaroline fosamil
- Bactericidal activity similar to cefotaxime
- Expanded Gram-positive coverage
- Active against:
- MRSA
- Multidrug-resistant Streptococcus pneumoniae
#### Licensed Uses
- Community-acquired pneumonia
- Complicated skin and soft-tissue infections
#### Note
- No clinical experience in MRSA pneumonia
---

# Special Cephalosporin
### Cefiderocol
- Siderophore cephalosporin
#### Use
- Serious infections due to Gram-negative aerobic organisms
- For patients with limited treatment options
- Often used when other antimicrobials have failed
