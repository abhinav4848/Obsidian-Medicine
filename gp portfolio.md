### Fitness to practise
Professionalism and protecting self and others from harm; including awareness of when an individual’s performance, conduct or health, or that of others, might put patients, themselves, or their colleagues at risk. 

Learning Outcomes
- Demonstrating the attitudes and behaviours expected of a good doctor
- Managing the factors that influence your performance
- Promoting health and wellbeing in yourself and colleagues

|Indicators of Potential Underperformance|Needs further development|Competent|Excellent|
|---|---|---|---|
|- Does not comply with accepted codes of professional practice. <br>- Fails to respect the requirements of organisations, such as meeting deadlines, producing documentation and observing contractual obligations.<br>- Does not recognise limits of own ability and experience.<br>- Is the subject of multiple complaints.<br>- Repeatedly fails to cope effectively with the demands of the job, such as dealing with stress or managing time.<br>- Has repeated unexplained or unplanned absence from professional commitments.|- Understands and follows the GMC's 'duties of a doctor' guidance. (As found in "[Good medical practice](https://www.gmc-uk.org/professional-standards/the-professional-standards/good-medical-practice/the-duties-of-medical-professionals-registered-with-the-gmc)")<br>- provided.<br>- Evaluates their clinical care and is able to justify actions to patients, colleagues, and professional bodies.<br>- Demonstrates insight into any personal physical or mental illness or habits which might interfere with the competent delivery of patient care.<br>- Identifies and notifies an appropriate person when their own or a colleague’s performance, conduct or health might be putting others at risk.<br>- Responds to complaints or performance issues appropriately.<br>- Monitors performance and demonstrates insight into their personal needs.<br>- Demonstrates awareness of the needs of colleagues.|- Demonstrates the accepted codes of practice to promote patient safety and effective team working.<br>- Reacts promptly, respectfully, and impartially when there are concerns about self or colleagues.<br>- Works within the limits of their own ability and expertise as a GP.<br>- Adopts a self-directed approach to learning, engaging with assessment.<br>- Encourages scrutiny performance.<br>- Promotes the wellbeing and health of all colleagues and staff, both individually and collectively.|

### Communicating and Consulting
Communicating with patients, the use of recognised consultation techniques, establishing and maintaining patient partnerships, managing challenging consultations, third-party consulting, the use of interpreters, and consulting modalities across the range of in-person and remote 

Learning Outcomes
- Establishing an effective partnership with the patient through a range of in-person and remote consulting modalities
- Managing the additional challenge of consultations with patients who have particular communication needs, or who have different languages, cultures, beliefs, and educational backgrounds, to your own
- Maintaining continuing relationships with patients, carers, and families

|---|---|---|---|
|- Does not establish rapport with the patient.<br>- Misses or ignores significant cues.<br>- Is unable to consult within time scales that are appropriate to the stage of training.<br>- Makes inappropriate assumptions about the patient's agenda.<br>- Has a blinkered approach and is unable to adapt the consultation, despite cues or new information.<br>- Uses stock phrases or inappropriate medical jargon rather than tailoring the language to the patient's needs and context.<br>- Does not give space and time to the patient when this is needed.<br>- Repeatedly ignores the input of the carers or families within consultations|- Consults to an acceptable standard but lacks focus and requires longer consultation times.<br>- Adopts a basic personalised approach to care.<br>- models or theories.<br>- Uses language and explanations that are technically correct but not consistently tailored to the needs and characteristics of the patient.<br>- Understands the need for effective consulting and developing an awareness of the wide range of consultation models that might be used.<br>- Takes steps to address barriers to communication, including use of interpreters.<br>- Develops a relationship with the patient that is effective but is focused on the problem rather than the patient.<br>- Elicits psychological and social information to place the patient’s problem in context.|- Uses the most appropriate mode of consultation, including in-person and remote, taking account of individual patient needs, preferences, and safety.<br>- Explores the patient's ### Decision-making and Diagnosis
Adopting a conscious, organised approach to making diagnosis and decisions that are tailored to the particular circumstances in which they are required. 

Learning Outcomes
- Adopting appropriate decision-making principles based on a shared understanding
- Using best available, current, valid, and relevant evidence

|Indicators of Potential Underperformance|Needs further development|Competent|Excellent|
|---|---|---|---|
|- Is indecisive, illogical, or incorrect in decision-making.<br>- Is dogmatic or closed to other ideas.<br>- Too frequently has late or missed diagnoses.<br>- Fails to consider serious possibilities.<br>- Ignores relevant guidelines.|- Generates differential diagnoses that may be too narrow or broad.<br>- Generates and tests appropriate hypotheses.<br>- Develops independent skills in decision-making and uses the support of others to confirm these are correct.<br>- Uses decision aids (such as algorithms and risk calculators) for straightforward clinical decisions.<br>- Justifies chosen options with evidence.<br>- Is aware of personal limitations in knowledge and experience.|- Makes diagnoses in a structured way using a problem-solving method.<br>- Thinks flexibly around problems, generating functional solutions.<br>- Demonstrates confidence in, and takes ownership of, own decisions while being aware of their own limitations.<br>- Demonstrates rapid and safe decision-making when managing urgent clinical situations and when it is appropriate to defer an action.<br>- Uses pattern recognition to identify diagnoses quickly, safely, and reliably.<br>- Keeps an open mind and is able to adjust and revise decisions and diagnoses when considering new relevant information.<br>- Addresses problems that present early and/or in an undifferentiated way by integrating all the available information to help generate a differential diagnosis.<br>- Uses an understanding of probability based on prevalence, incidence, and natural history of illness to aid decision-making.|- Understands the benefits and limitations of pattern recognition and an analytical approach, and knows how to use them concurrently.<br>- Reflects appropriately on complex decisions and develops mechanisms to be comfortable with these choices.<br>- Justifies discretionary judgement, no longer relying on rules and protocols in situations of uncertainty or complexity, for example in patients with multiple problems.|
