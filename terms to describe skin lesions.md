# 🔹 Primary Skin Lesions (Flat & Raised)
### Flat lesions
- Macule – Flat, non-palpable change in colour <1 cm
- Patch – Flat, non-palpable colour change >1 cm
---
### Raised / Palpable lesions
- Papule – Solid, raised lesion <1 cm
- Plaque – Raised, flat-topped lesion >1 cm (often coalesced papules)
- Nodule – Solid, deeper lesion (dermis/subcutis), usually >1 cm
- Tumour – Large solid mass (benign or malignant)
---
### Fluid-filled lesions
- Vesicle – Clear fluid-filled lesion <1 cm
- Bulla – Clear fluid-filled lesion >1 cm
- Pustule – Pus-filled lesion
- Abscess – Localised collection of pus in dermis/subcutis
- Cyst – Epithelial-lined cavity with fluid or semi-solid material
---
# 🔹 Secondary Lesions (Evolution / Trauma)
- Scale – Flakes of stratum corneum
- Crust – Dried serum/blood/pus
- Erosion – Superficial loss of epidermis (no scar)
- Ulcer – Full thickness epidermal loss ± dermis (may scar)
- Excoriation – Linear erosion from scratching
- Fissure – Linear crack in skin
- Lichenification – Thickened skin with exaggerated markings (chronic rubbing)
- Atrophy – Thinning of skin
- Scar (cicatrix) – Fibrous tissue replacement
- Keloid – Excess scar extending beyond original wound
- Eschar – Black necrotic crust

---
# 🔹 Vascular Lesions
- Petechiae – Non-blanching red/purple macules <5mm
- Purpura – Non-blanching lesions 5-10 mm
- Ecchymosis – Large purpuric patch (bruise) > 1cm
- Telangiectasia – Dilated superficial vessels
- Angioma – Benign vascular proliferation
- Haematoma – Localised blood collection

---
# 🔹 Other Descriptive Terms
- Wheal – Transient, oedematous plaque (e.g. urticaria)
- Comedone – Plugged pilosebaceous unit
	- Open (blackhead)
	- Closed (whitehead)
- Burrow – Linear tunnel in epidermis (e.g. scabies)
- Sinus – Channel connecting deep infection to surface
- Fistula – Abnormal tract between two epithelial surfaces

---
# 🔹 Surface & Texture Descriptors
- Verrucous – Wart-like
- Hyperkeratotic – Thickened stratum corneum
- Umbilicated – Central depression
- Annular – Ring-shaped
- Targetoid – Target-like (e.g. erythema multiforme)
- Serpiginous – Wavy/creeping border
- Grouped – Clustered lesions
- Confluent – Merged together

---
# 🔹 Colour Terms
- Erythematous – Red

- Hyperpigmented
