# Chronic Shortness of Breath
See [[mrc dyspnoea scale]]. 

|                                                 |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| [[copd\|chronic obstructive pulmonary disease]] | Seen invariably in smokers  <br>Chronic productive cough is typical <br>Features of right [[heart failure]] may be seen                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Heart failure                                   | A history of ischaemic heart disease or [[hypertension ]]may be present  <br>Orthopnoea and paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea are characteristic  <br>Bi-basal crackles and a third heart sound (S3) are the most reliable features of left-sided failure. <br>Right heart failure causes peripheral oedema and a raised JVP |
| [[Asthma]]                                      | Cough, wheeze and shortness of breath are typical  <br>Symptoms are often worse at night and may be precipitated by cold weather or exercise  <br>Associated with hay fever and eczema                                                                                                                                   |
| Aortic stenosis                                 | Chest pain, SOB and syncope seen in symptomatic patients.<br>An ESM (ejection systolic murmur) radiating to the neck. and narrow pulse pressure are found on examination                                                                                                                                                 |
| Recurrent pulmonary emboli                      | There may be a history of predisposing factors e.g. Malignancy  <br>Pleuritic chest pain, and haemoptysis may be seen but symptoms are often vague  <br>Tachycardia and tachypnoea are common in the acute situation  <br>Symptoms of right heart failure may develop in severe cases.                                   |
| [[cancer- lung\|Lung Cancer]]                   | Normally seen in smokers  <br>Haemoptysis, chronic cough or unresolving infection are common presentations <br>Systemic symptoms e.g. Weight loss and anorexia                                                                                                                                                           |
| [[lung fibrosis\|Pulmonary fibrosis]]           | Progressive shortness of breath may be the only symptom  <br>Fine bi-basal crackles are typical  <br>Spirometry shows a [[spirometry#Restrictive Lung Diseases\|restrictive pattern]].                                                                                                                                   |
| [[Bronchiectasis]]                              | Affected patients may produce large amounts of purulent sputum  <br>Patients may have a history of previous infections (e.g. [[Tuberculosis]], measles), bronchial obstruction or ciliary dyskinetic syndromes e.g. Kartagener's syndrome                                                                                |
| Anaemia                                         | There may be a history of gastrointestinal symptoms  <br>Pallor may be seen on examination                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| Obesity                                         | Obese patients tend to be more SOB due to the increased work of activity                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
