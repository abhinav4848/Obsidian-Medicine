# Arm

|                          |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Carpal tunnel            | Median nerve.<br>Transverse carpal ligament/Flexor retinaculum/Anterior annular ligament.<br>**Risk factor**: Pregnancy (due to fluid retention), too tight cast.<br><br>Can cause awakening from sleep. <br>Tinel test not always +ve.<br><br>**Rx:**<br>Cut the flexor retinaculum.<br>Pregnant: Wear splint till delivery.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Colle's                  | Commonly due to osteoporosis<br>Dinner fork deformity. Median nerve injury<br>Distal radius dorsally/posteriorly displaced, dorsal angulated.<br><br>Do DEXA to assess future risk in case of [[osteoporosis]].<br><br>Rx: In both cases, elevate with a sling.<br><br>Young:<br>Above elbow backslab cast (A type of cast that is non-circumferential, only covering the posterior aspect of a limb, in order to permit swelling).<br><br>Elderly:<br>Closed reduction + POP below elbow, then # clinic referral.<br>If intraarticular fracture/incongruity: ORIF.<br>If non-displaced: Wrist splint. |
| Galeazzi                 | Distal RUJ dislocation, # distal 1/3rd radius.<br>![[monteggia vs galeazzi fracture.jpg]]                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Golfer's elbow           | Medial epicondylitis. All flexors to fingers, & pronator are affected.<br><br>Seen in baseball players, construction injury, plumber injury                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Monteggia                | Radial nerve affected. Medial bone (ulna) fracture.<br><br>Dislocation of head of radius, # proximal 1/3rd ulna.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Radius head vs neck #    | Head: more common in adults (> 16yo).<br>Neck: more common in children (< 16yo).<br><br>Radial head, neck, distal humerus # occur due to fall on outstretched hand.<br>Passive rotation of elbow causes pain.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Smith                    | Garden spade deformity, reverse colle's fracture.<br><br>Distal radius anteriorly displaced.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Supracondylar fracture   | Angulated supracondylar fracture of humerus<br>4 - 10 yo child falls on outstretched arm + absent radial/brachial pulse.<br><br>Keeping the elbow in extension after injury prevents exacerbating brachial artery damage from time of injury in supracondylar #.<br><br>If posterolateral displacement: Median nerve injury.<br>If posteromedial displacement: Radial nerve injury<br><br>Ecchymosis in antecubital fossa.                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Tennis Elbow             | Lateral epicondylitis<br>Affects wrist extension.<br>Occurs due to overuse.<br>Extend elbow, you get pain if you<br>- Extend wrist<br>- Supination of forearm                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Torus/buckle fracture    | Fall on outstretched hand in child.<br><br>Bulging/buckling of cortex of long bone due to incomplete # -> Occurs at junction of Metaphysis & Diaphysis. Harder diaphysis pushed into softer metaphysis -> Bulging & swelling<br><br>E.g. Distal radius<br>Children 4 - 10yo.<br><br>Self limiting.<br>Can be managed with splinting & immobilization rather than cast.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Cubitus Varus            | Gunstock deformity, due to malunion in supracondylar # of humerus.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |

In forearm > leg.<br>- Torus fracture/buckle fracture: It's a buckling fracture of distal radius. i.e. It's a bent. Not a deformity.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |

# Hand

|                                          |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |                                                                   |
| ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Boutonniere deformity                    | Flexion of PIP joint<br><br>Hyperextension of DIP joint.<br><br>Due to damage to central slip extensor tendon overlying PIP joint.  <br>  <br>Can be seen in [[Rheumatoid arthritis]].                                                                                                                                                                                                         | ![[boutonniere's deformity.png]]                                  |
| De Quervain tenosynovitis                | Radial styloid tenosynovitis, cell-phone thumb<br><br>Washerwoman thumb, Mommy thumb.<br><br>Pain under root of thumb.<br><br>Superficial branch of radial nerve entrapped.                                                                                                                                                                                                                    | ![[de quervain synovitis.jpg]]                                    |
| Dupuytren's contracture                  | Fixed forward curvature of >=1 fingers (usually 4th & 5th).<br><br>Due to Thickened fibrous tissue in palmar fascia (Fibrous connection b/w finger tendons & skin of palm)<br><br>Causes: Manual labour, family h/o, [[phenytoin]], alcoholic liver disease, trauma to hand, DM, Smoking.<br><br>Rx: Fasciotomy.                                                                               | ![[dupuytren's contracture.png]]                                  |
| Gamekeeper thumb                         | Skier thumb.<br><br>Injury to ulnar collateral ligament.<br><br>Weakness & pain when grasping things with thumb. Tenderness over MCP joints.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   | ![[skier's thumb.png]]                                            |
| Jersey finger                            | Forced extension of flexed finger causing tearing of FDP.<br><br>Athlete unable to flex DIP.<br><br>E.g.: Grabbing opponent's jersey in a game.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                | ![[jersey finger.png]]                                            |
| Mallet Finger                            | Due to forced flexion of extended finger causing avulsion of extensor digitorum tendon at DIP -> flexed bent finger.<br><br>E.g.: ball hits an outstretched finger.<br><br>See [[rheumatoid arthritis]].                                                                                                                                                                                       | ![[mallet Finger.png]] ![[rheumatoid arthritis hand changes.png]] |
| Scaphoid fracture                        | Fall/hit on outstretched hand -> forced dorsiflexion of hand.<br><br>Pronation followed by ulnar deviation produces pain in the anatomical snuff box/root of thumb.<br><br>Axial compression of thumb also produces pain.<br><br>  <br>if x-ray +ve: scaphoid cast for 6 weeks<br><br>If x-ray -ve: scaphoid cast (basically immobilize), and repeat x-ray in 2 weeks.                         |                                                                   |
| Trigger finger (stenosing tenosynovitis) | It occurs when inflammation or nodules develop in the flexor tendon sheath of a finger, causing pain and difficulty in movement<br><br>C/F<br>1. Thumb, middle, ring fingers.<br>2. Stiffness/getting stuck of finger and snapping (click, like a trigger) when extending flexed digit.<br>3. Nodule felt at base of affected finger.<br><br>Rx: Steroid injection + finger splint afterwards. | ![[trigger finger (stenosing tenosynovitis).png]]                 |
