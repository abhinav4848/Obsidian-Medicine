Common in someone with other autoimmune disease like
- T1DM,
- Addison's,
- Pernicious anaemia (megaloblastic anaemia),
- Thyroid disease (Hashimoto),
- alopecia areata

# Clinical Features
- symmetrical, well-demarcated depigmented patches on the dorsum of hands is classic for vitiligo.
- the peripheries tend to be most affected
- trauma may precipitate new lesions ([[koebner's phenomenon]]). 

## Differentials
**Leukopaenia-induced fungal infection** is incorrect. Although carbimazole can cause leukopaenia, which could theoretically predispose to fungal infections, fungal infections typically present with scaling, erythema, and pruritus rather than simple depigmentation. Additionally, fungal infections would likely respond, at least partially, to topical treatments like emollients/hydrocortisone. 

# Management
Prevent: Daily sunscreen to affected areas.  
OD potent topical steroid could reverse changes if applied early.
