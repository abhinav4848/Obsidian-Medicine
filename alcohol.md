# Wernicke Encephalopathy
Triad: #triad - Ophthalmoplegia, Ataxia, Confusion. 
Due to B1 deficiency. 

- Oculomotor dysfunction:
	- Ophthalmoplegia: lateral rectus palsy, conjugate gaze palsy
	- Nystagmus
- Gait Ataxia 
- Encephalopathy:
	- Confusion, disorientation, indifference, and inattentiveness
- Peripheral sensory neuropathy 

Give Pabrinex which has Vit B1 & Vit C.

**Else**: Wernicke-Korsakoff
- Amnesia (anterograde & Retrograde)
- Confabulation

# Hepatic encephalopathy
#### Signs of liver cell failure
Icterus, parotid swelling, spider Naevi, Palmar erythema, [[gynaecomastia]] (areola painful, >5cm), testicular atrophy, breast atrophy in females, loss of pubic and axillary hair, [[upper limb ortho problems|Dupuytren's contracture]], bleeding tendencies, pedal edema, hepatic encephalopathy, flapping tremors- (CCF, uraemia, CO2 narcosis), (also know that there can be constructional apraxia, massive ascites can cause pleural effusion)
#### Hepatic encephalopathy (West haven criteria)
Grade 0: no detectable changes
Grade 1: altered sensation, shortened attention span, daytime somnolence
Grade 2: lethargy, slurred speech, agitated, asterixis
Grade 3: confused, stupor
Grade 4: coma
##### Rx
Reducing the nitrogen load from the gut using lactulose or bowel enemas. Some antibiotics like neomycin, [[metronidazole]] and rifaximin which reduce amino acid production may help.
