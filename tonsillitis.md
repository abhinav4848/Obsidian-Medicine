Viral tonsillitis disappears in 10 days

# When to give antibiotics
1. Systemic upset due to sore throat
2. u/l peri-tonsillitis
3. h/o Rheumatic fever
4. Increased risk from acute infection (DM/immunodeficiency)
5. Centor >=3

Centor criteria to know if viral or bacterial
	If there's 3 out of 4, suspect Streptococcus & give antibiotics.
	Check patient's allergy status first.
	No Allergy: Phenoxymethylpenicillin- Penicillin V for 10 days. #abx
	*Pen V also used in [[scarlet Fever]]*
	Pen allergic: 5 days of erythromycin or clarithromycin

**Tonsillectomy**: Done if SIGN criteria satisfied
\> 7 episodes in one year
\> 5 episodes per year for 2 years  
\> 3 episodes per year for 3 years  

## Complication of Tonsillectomy
1ry bleeding is within 24 hrs. Return to theatre.
If tonsillectomy causes 2ry bleeding, usually it's due to infection leading to vessel erosion: Admit & give iv antibiotics.
If above two not indicated, give Paracetamol/Ibuprofen (Supportive).

# Complication of Tonsillitis
If abx not given in acute bacterial tonsillitis, can become quinsy. 
Quinsy (peritonsillar abscess) is a rare complication and a medical #emergency due to risk of airway compromise.
