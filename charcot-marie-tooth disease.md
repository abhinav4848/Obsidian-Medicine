Charcot-Marie-Tooth Disease is the most common hereditary peripheral neuropathy: [[autosomal inheritance|autosomal dominant]]. 

It results in a predominantly motor loss. Causes distal muscle weakness and wasting, particularly affecting the peroneal muscles leading to foot drop. There is no cure, and management is focused on physical and occupational therapy.  
# Features
- Slow progression over years. 
- There may be a history of frequently sprained ankles
- Foot drop b/l
- High-arched feet (pes cavus)
- Hammer toes
- Distal muscle weakness
- Distal muscle atrophy but proximal muscles preserved (inverted champagne bottle / stork leg appearance)
- Hyporeflexia (absent ankle reflexes)

# DDx
- [[motor neurone disease]] presents in late adulthood (40-70yo) & has both UMN & LMN signs. 
- **Inclusion body myositis** is an inflammatory muscle disease that typically affects older adults (over 50 years) and has a predilection for the quadriceps and finger flexors rather than distal leg muscles. It would not cause the high-arched feet or absent reflexes seen in CMT, and it's not typically hereditary.
