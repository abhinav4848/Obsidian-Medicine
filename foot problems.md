# Morton's neuroma
Most commonly occurs in women aged 40–50 who wear high heeled shoes. The pain is due to entrapment of the interdigital nerve between the 3rd/4th metatarsal heads. However, other interspaces can be involved.

Symptoms include gradual onset of sudden attacks of pain or paraesthesia on walking. Clinical examination is the mainstay of diagnosis. There is localised tenderness over the site of the neuroma. There may be diminished sensation in the affected cleft. Compression of the forefoot with pressure at the intermetatarsal space often elicits a click as the nerve is pushed between the metatarsals (termed a Mulder's click). Ultrasound scans are considered by many the diagnostic test of choice. Excision is the recommended treatment
# Metatarsal stress fracture
 usually felt on the dorsal aspect of the foot over the affected metatarsal bone. There may be diffuse swelling of the skin of the forefoot. Sharp pain in the forefoot is aggravated by walking and running.
# Sesamoiditis
Typically occurs in young people with gradual onset of pain behind the first metatarsal head which can be caused by dancing or running.
# Bursitis
A bursa is a small fluid filled sac which protects pressure points between bones, muscles and tendons. They occur naturally (there are over 150 throughout the body) and help the joints and tendons to move easily. However, they can become inflamed resulting in bursitis.

Bursitis is a common cause of pain in the forefoot. It occurs commonly in the forefoot at the first and second metatarsal phalangeal joints. Bursitis can occur with injuries, overuse and repetitive stress. Arthritis and [[gout]] may also cause it.
# Bunion (Hallux Valgus)
[NHS Leaflet](https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/bunions/)
A bunion is a progressive deformity of the first MTP joint in which the great toe deviates laterally towards the second toe, with medial prominence of the first metatarsal head.

More common in women and associated with tight or narrow footwear. There is often a hereditary component.

**Symptoms:**
- Pain over the medial aspect of the first MTP joint
- Swelling and redness over the bony prominence
- Difficulty wearing shoes
- Cosmetic concern

**Examination findings:**
- Visible lateral deviation of the hallux
- Medial bony prominence
- Tenderness over the first MTP joint
- May coexist with bursitis over the bunion

Pain is typically mechanical and worsened by footwear.

# Others
### Plantar fasciitis
Most common cause of heel pain. Pain is felt on the plantar aspect of the heel, especially first thing in the morning or after rest.
### Achilles tendinopathy
Posterior heel pain and stiffness, worse with activity or on first steps after rest.
### Tarsal tunnel syndrome
Entrapment of the posterior tibial nerve. Burning pain or paraesthesia on the medial ankle radiating into the sole.
### Hallux rigidus
Degenerative arthritis of the first MTP joint. Pain and stiffness at the base of the big toe, especially on dorsiflexion.
### Freiberg’s disease
Avascular necrosis of the second metatarsal head. Adolescent females. Forefoot pain localised to the 2nd MTP.
### Claw/hammer toes
Pain over dorsal PIP joints due to shoe pressure; often associated with callus formation.**
