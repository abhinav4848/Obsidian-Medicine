### Risk Factors / Predisposing Factors
- [[antipsychotic drugs|Antipsychotics]] reduce the likelihood of delirium.
- [[anticholinergics]] predispose to delirium.
- [[dementia]] lowers the threshold for delirium, and delirium can also worsen dementia.

## Differential Diagnosis
It is important to differentiate hypoactive delirium from catatonia, as treatment differs.
- Catatonia treatment:
    - Lorazepam
    - Midazolam

# Management
Underlying cause
### Antipsychotics
[[antipsychotic drugs|Antipsychotics]] (e.g. Haloperidol, Olanzapine, Quetiapine):
- Improve symptoms in ~75% of delirium cases, regardless of subtype.
- Do not treat the underlying cause.
- Do not shorten delirium duration.
- Used mainly for symptom control when non-pharmacological measures fail.
### Benzodiazepines
Although benzodiazepines can paradoxically worsen agitation, they are preferred in specific situations over antipsychotics (e.g. when dopamine already low)
- Delirium due to [[alcohol]] withdrawal
- [[extrapyramidal side-effects|Acute dopamine depletion syndrome]]
- [[parkinson's disease|Parkinson's disease]] 
- Lewy Body Dementia
- Severe EPS
