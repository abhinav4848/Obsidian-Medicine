# 🧠 Quick memory hook
- Routine = normal clinical need
- Prior approval = allowed but controlled
- IFR = exceptional case funding
- Private = cosmetic/no medical need

---
## 🟢 1. Routine referral (standard NHS pathway)
Use when:
- Condition is **medically indicated and common**
- Fits normal specialist criteria
- No special funding approval needed

Examples:
- Suspected cancer → urgent 2WW pathway
- Cataract with visual impairment
- Most ENT, dermatology, rheumatology referrals

👉 **Key idea:** "Clinically appropriate → just refer"

---

## 🟡 2. Prior approval pathway (commissioning approval before treatment)
Use when:
- Treatment is **restricted or cost-controlled**
- Usually **only funded if strict criteria met**
- Common in "borderline cosmetic/functional" cases

Examples:
- Some **minor dermatology procedures** (skin tags, benign lesions)
- Varicose vein procedures (depending on severity)
- Weight loss surgery criteria-based referrals in some regions

👉 **Key idea:** "Allowed, but needs permission first"

---

## 🔵 3. Individual Funding Request (IFR)
Use when:
- Treatment is **NOT normally funded by NHS**
- Patient has **exceptional clinical circumstances**
- Must prove "this patient is different from the average case"

Examples:
- Rare cancer treatments not routinely commissioned
- Off-label or very expensive drugs not in policy
- Exceptional disability or failed standard pathways

👉 **Key idea:** "Not funded unless this patient is exceptional"

---

## 🔴 4. Self-funded (private only)
Use when:
- Procedure is **purely cosmetic**
- No functional or medical indication
- NHS explicitly does not fund it

Examples:
- Purely cosmetic mole removal
- Cosmetic Botox
- Hair transplant (androgenetic alopecia usually)

👉 **Key idea:** "Aesthetic only → not NHS responsibility"
