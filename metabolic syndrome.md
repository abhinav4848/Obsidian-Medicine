For a diagnosis of metabolic syndrome at least 3 of the following should be identified:  
- elevated waist circumference: men > 102 cm, women > 88 cm
- elevated triglycerides: > 1.7 mmol/L
- reduced HDL: < 1.03 mmol/L in males and < 1.29 mmol/L in females
- raised blood pressure: > 130/85 mmHg, or active treatment of hypertension
- raised fasting plasma glucose > 5.6 mmol/L, or previously diagnosed type 2 diabetes

Pathophysiological factor is insulin resistance. 
CVD risk factors should be considered in everyone, not just those with metabolic problems.
Raised LDL is not a criterion. Reduced LDL & increased HDL are. 
Diagnosis can be made without patient weight.
