# Onychomycosis
aka tinea unguium due to 
- dermatophytes: *trichophyton rubrum* (toenails)- Most common
- Yeasts: candida (fingernails)
- Non-dermatophyte moulds
##### Symptoms
- nail thickening
- discolouration
- subungual debris

DDx: [[psoriasis]], repeated trauma, [[lichen planus]], yellow nail syndrome
# Investigation
This should be confirmed with laboratory testing before either topical or oral treatment is prescribed. Although the most likely cause would be onychomycosis (fungal nail infection), it could just as well be due to another condition such as psoriasis so it would be beneficial to confirm fungal involvement. 
- Send nail clipping +/- nail scraping for all patients if antifungal treatment is being considered. False-negative rate for cultures are around 30%, so repeat samples may need to be sent if the clinical suspicion is high
# Treatment
Don't need treating if asymptomatic & patient not bothered by the appearance. 
- Topical treatments are generally not ideal for nail infections.  
- If extensive involvement due to a dermatophyte, oral terbinafine. 6weeks to 23 months for fingernail, 3-6 month for toenail
- if extensive due to candida- Itraconazole oral. *[[monitoring bloods with drugs|monitor]] LFT if Itraconazole continued longer than a month.*
- Use topical steroid if nail clipping doesn't show fungus, but rather indicates inflammatory dermatosis such as psoriasis.
