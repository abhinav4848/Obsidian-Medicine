# Routine tests
**BP**: checked at every visit. Particularly at 28 weeks as this is when [[pre-eclampsia]] commonly develops. 

**Blood**: 
- Blood group & Rh status
- Antibody screen
- Full blood count
- **HIV**, **Hepatitis B**, **Syphilis**

**Urine dipstick**: Asymptomatic bacteriuria, proteinuria (pre-eclampsia), glycosuria ([[diabetes- gestational|GDM]]), UTI
Other: [[down's syndrome]], [[childhood syndromes|Edward syndrome]], patau.
### Bacteriuria
If a woman has had asymptomatic bacteriuria in pregnancy, a repeat MSU is required at all subsequent antenatal appointments.
- Prophylactic antibiotics in labour and delivery are only needed if a ==group B streptococcus== is isolated. The antenatal care service will need to be informed so that they can arrange prophylactic antibiotics for labour and delivery.
- A woman does not need to be on prophylactic antibiotics until delivery if she is found to have asymptomatic bacteriuria. She may, however, be treated with a short course of antibiotics if an organism is isolated.
- Important because it can cause increased risk of [[pyelonephritis]], preterm birth. 
- No need for a renal USS. 
- See about [[trimethoprim]] & [[nitrofurantoin]]

NICE issued guidelines on routine care for the healthy pregnant woman in March 2008. They recommend:  
- 10 antenatal visits in the first pregnancy if uncomplicated
- 7 antenatal visits in subsequent pregnancies if uncomplicated
- women do not need to be seen by a consultant if the pregnancy is uncomplicated

| **Gestation**                     | **Purpose of visit**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 8 - 12 weeks (ideally < 10 weeks) | Booking visit  <br><br>- general information e.g. diet, alcohol, smoking, folic acid, vitamin D, antenatal classes<br>- BP, urine dipstick, check BMI<br><br>Booking bloods/urine  <br><br>- FBC, blood group, rhesus status, red cell alloantibodies, haemoglobinopathies<br>- hepatitis B, syphilis<br>- HIV test is offered to all women<br>- urine culture to detect asymptomatic bacteriuria |
| 10 - 13+6 weeks                   | Dating **scan**: Early scan to confirm dates, exclude multiple pregnancy                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| 11 - 13+6 weeks                   | [[down's syndrome]] screening including nuchal **scan**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| 16 weeks                          | Information on the anomaly and the blood results. If Hb < 11 g/dl consider iron <br>Routine care: BP and urine dipstick                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| 18 - 20+6 weeks                   | Anomaly **scan**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| 25 weeks (only if primip)         | Routine care: BP, urine dipstick, symphysis-fundal height (SFH)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| 28 weeks⭐                         | Routine care: BP, urine dipstick, SFH  <br>Second screen for anaemia and atypical red cell alloantibodies. If Hb < 10.5 g/dl consider iron  <br>First dose of anti-D prophylaxis to rhesus negative women                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| 31 weeks (only if primip)         | Routine care as above                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| 34 weeks                          | Routine care as above  <br>Second dose of anti-D prophylaxis to rhesus negative women\*  <br>Information on labour and birth plan                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| 36 weeks                          | Routine care as above  <br>Check presentation - offer external cephalic version if indicated  <br>Information on breast feeding, vitamin K, 'baby-blues'                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| 38 weeks                          | Routine care as above                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| 40 weeks (only if primip)         | Routine care as above  <br>Discussion about options for prolonged pregnancy                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| 41 weeks                          | Routine care as above  <br>Discuss labour plans and possibility of induction                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
\*the evidence base suggests that there is little difference in the efficacy of single-dose (at 28 weeks) and double-dose regimes (at 28 & 34 weeks). For this reason the RCOG in 2011 advised that either regime could be used 'depending on local factors'
