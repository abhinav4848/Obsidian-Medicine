| Diagnosis                                                                          | Notes                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| [[aspergilloma]]                                                                   | Often past history of [[tuberculosis]].<br>Haemoptysis may be severe<br>Chest x-ray shows rounded opacity                                                                               |
| [[bronchiectasis]]                                                                 | Usually long history of cough and daily purulent sputum production                                                                                                                      |
| Goodpasture's syndrome                                                             | Haemoptysis<br>Systemically unwell: fever, nausea<br>Glomerulonephritis                                                                                                                 |
| Granulomatosis with polyangiitis ([[granulomatosis with polyangiitis\|Wegener's]]) | Upper respiratory tract: epistaxis, sinusitis, nasal crusting<br>Lower respiratory tract: dyspnoea, haemoptysis<br>Glomerulonephritis<br>Saddle-shape nose deformity                    |
| Lower respiratory tract infection                                                  | Usually acute history of purulent cough                                                                                                                                                 |
| [[cancer- lung\|Lung Cancer]]                                                      | History of smoking<br>A patient who is >= 40 years old presenting with unexplained haemoptysis should get 2ww (smoking optional)<br><br>*Symptoms of malignancy: weight loss, anorexia* |
| Mitral stenosis                                                                    | Dyspnoea<br>Atrial fibrillation<br>Malar flush on cheeks<br>Mid-diastolic murmur                                                                                                        |
| Pulmonary embolism                                                                 | Pleuritic chest pain<br>Tachycardia, tachypnoea                                                                                                                                         |
| Pulmonary oedema                                                                   | Dyspnoea<br>Bi-basal crackles and S3 are the most reliable signs                                                                                                                        |
| Tuberculosis                                                                       | Fever, night sweats, anorexia, weight loss                                                                                                                                              |
