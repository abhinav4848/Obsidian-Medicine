A type of skin angioma found in the distribution of the superior vena cava. They can occur spontaneously, but multiple lesions are abnormal and may indicate excess oestrogen - typically due to
1. childhood
2. liver cirrhosis (as the liver processes oestrogens),
3. pregnancy or
4. oestrogen-containing medications ([[COCP]]).

# Clinical Feature
Lesions blanch under pressure.

Press on it & watch it fill
- Fills from centre outwards: Spider naevi
- Fills from outside to in: Telangiectasia.
