Apparent abnormalities in the lower limbs of developing children can cause parental concern and is a common reason for presentation to primary care, often resulting in referral to secondary care.  

*(see [[lower limb ortho problems]]*)

# Common presentations

| Description                   | Typical age of presentation | Signs                                                                                                 | Notes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Flat feet (pes planus)**    | All ages                    | Absent medial arch on standing                                                                        | Typically resolves b/w 4-8yo<br>Orthotics not recommended  <br>Parental reassurance appropriate                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **In toeing**                 | 1st year                    | [Orthobullets- Femoral anteversion](https://www.orthobullets.com/pediatrics/4059/femoral-anteversion) | **Possible causes:** <br><br>- metatarsus adductus: abnormal heel bisector line. 90% of cases resolve spontaneously, severe/persistent cases may require serial casting<br><br>- internal tibial torsion: difference b/w thigh and foot/ankle alignment: resolves in the vast majority<br><br>- femoral anteversion: 'W' sitting position: resolves in around 80% by adolescence, surgical intervention in the remaining not usually advised |
| **Out toeing**                | All ages                    |                                                                                                       | Common in early infancy and usually resolves by the age of 2 years  <br>Usually due to external tibial torsion  <br>Intervention may be appropriate if doesn't resolve as increases risk of patellofemoral pain                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Bow legs (genu varum)**     | 1st-2nd year                | Increased intercondylar distance                                                                      | Typically resolves by the age of 4-5 years                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Knock knees (genu valgum)** | 3rd-4th year                | Increased intermalleolar distance                                                                     | Typically resolves spontaneously                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
