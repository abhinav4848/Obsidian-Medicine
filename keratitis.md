Bacterial: common with contact lens wearers. Red eye, photophobia, FB sensation. White spot on pen light. <br><br>Viral: as above, but watery discharge. Faint branching opacity on pen light

# Cause
- bacterial: Staph, pseudomonas in contact lens wearers
- fungal
- amoebic: pain classically out of proportion to the findings. 
- parasitic: onchocercal keratitis (river blindness)
- viral: herpes simplex keratitis
- environmental: photokeratitis: welder's arc, exposure keratitis, contact lens acute red eye (CLARE)

# Clinical Features
- red eye: pain and erythema
- photophobia
- foreign body, gritty sensation
- hypopyon may be seen

# Investigation
Contact lens wearers: Refer same day for slit-lamp
