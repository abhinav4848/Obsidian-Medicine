# LTOT
LTOT: long term O2 therapy for 15 hrs. a day
## When to assess need for LTOT
- [[spirometry|FEV1]] <30% of predicted. i.e. Very severe airflow obstruction
- SpO2 < 92% on room air
- Cyanosis
- Polycythemia
- Peripheral edema
- Raised JVP
## Conditions for assessment
- Patient should be stable > 5 weeks since last [[COPD]] exacerbation
- Therapy for COPD is fully optimized
- 2 sets ABG 3 weeks apart show him hypoxic

## Offer LTOT if
pO2 < 7.3 kPa alone, or 
pO2 of 7.3 - 8 kPa & any of:
- Secondary polycythemia ([See UHL Leicester Haemat pathways](https://www.uhleicester.nhs.uk/services/haematology/haematology-referral-guidelines/))
- Peripheral edema
- Pulmonary HTN
Don't offer LTOT to people who continue to smoke despite being offered smoking cessation advice and treatment, and referral to specialist stop smoking services.

If already on LTOT but still breathless -> Prednisolone/nebulized NS to loosen secretions. 
Can also give carbocsteine for secretions although excess drug can cause excessive loose secretions. 
LVR (lung volume recruitment) bags help get enough air in to generate a cough.
