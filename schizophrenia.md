Chronic psychiatric disorder where [[psychosis]] is its core feature. 
Patient has delusion, hallucination, disorders of thought perception. 

Thought insertion is a first rank symptom of schizophrenia.
# Risk of developing schizophrenia 
- monozygotic twin has schizophrenia = 50%
- parent has schizophrenia = 10-15%
- sibling / dizygotic twin has schizophrenia = 10%
- no relatives with schizophrenia = 1%
### Other selected risk factors for psychotic disorders include:  
- [[afro-caribbean|Afro Caribbean]] ethnicity - RR 5.4
- Migration - RR 2.9
- Urban environment- RR 2.4
- Cannabis use - RR 1.4
# Prognosis
Factors associated with poor prognosis  
- strong family history
- gradual onset
	- acute onset presents early. As such, treated early and as a result can lead to better outcome.
- low IQ
- long prodromal phase of social withdrawal
- lack of obvious precipitant
- younger age of onset (especially in males)
- male sex
- prominent negative symptoms (blunted affect, avolition, alogia)
	- Avolition is a significant lack of motivation to initiate or sustain goal-directed activities, often linked to mental health conditions rather than laziness.
