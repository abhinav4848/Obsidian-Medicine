# Symptoms
- Fatigue
- sleep disturbance
- Generalised aching- Chronic widespread pain + multiple muscular tender points
- Morning stiffness
- Poor concentration or cognitive dysfunction (“fibro fog”) 
- Weight gain
- Normal Blood tests

 In the absence of a well-defined underlying organic disease. 
 Majority of patients present with both myofascial tender points and associated somatic symptoms
#### Somatic symptoms: 
- Somatic symptoms are physical symptoms that originate from how the nervous system processes signals, rather than from clear structural disease or tissue damage. 
	- Real physical symptoms without an adequate structural or biochemical explanation, caused by altered central processing of sensation, pain, or autonomic function.
	- Key point: the symptoms are real — not imagined, not “put on”.
- Pain related somatic symptoms: [[irritable bowel syndrome|IBS]], migraine headaches, severe menstrual pain, lower urinary tract symptoms, myofascial facial pain, and temporomandibular pain are seen in FM

Explain to patients: “Your nerves have become over-sensitive, so normal signals are being experienced as pain or discomfort. The symptoms are real, but the problem is with how signals are processed, not with damage.”

# Management
[GP Notebook- Fibro Management](https://gpnotebook.com/en-GB/pages/rheumatology/fibromyalgia-syndrome-fms/management)
#### Core Principle
- No cure → management focuses on symptom control and function
- No gold standard → use a multimodal, individualised approach
- Tailor treatment to symptoms with regular review and follow-up
- Encourage active patient involvement and goal-setting

#### Non-Pharmacological
Aerobic exercises, balneotherapy (heater pool/spa), acupuncture, CBT

#### Pharmacological
Analgesics: Paracetamol, NSAID
Opioids: Codeine not helpful. Tramadol may help. 
Antidepressants: Amitriptyline, Fluoxetine, Paroxetine- Improve pain, sleep, fatigue, mood
Anticonvulsants: Pregabalin effective, Gabapentin used sometimes but neither licensed.
