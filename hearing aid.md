# Hearing Aids
- Elderly patient with behind-the-ear hearing aid
- Can insert it fine
- No whistling (so good fit, no feedback)
- Tubing is bendy (not blocked/hardened)
## Setting
Behind-the-ear aids often have modes:
- M = Microphone (normal hearing mode)
- T = Telecoil (for loop systems, e.g. in theatres/churches)
- O = Off
## Problems
- If they’re struggling to hear, they may have accidentally switched to T or O
- Most common fix → return to normal microphone mode

# Cochlear Implant
Can be offered for severe-to-profound hearing loss.
### Suitability
- In children, [[hearing test in children|audiological assessment]] and/or difficulty developing basic auditory skills.
- In adults, patients should have completed a trial of appropriate hearing aids for at least 3 months which they have been objectively demonstrated to receive limited or no benefit from.

patients should get 5 yearly [[immunisation in adults|Pneumococcal polysaccharide vaccine]] thereafter.
