# Key clues:
- 70 years old + smoker + hypertensive → strong atherosclerotic risk factors
- Pain after eating (postprandial pain) → classic for reduced blood flow to the gut when demand increases -> Intestinal angina
- No response to Lansoprazole → argues against peptic ulcer disease or GERD
### Typical associated features (often tested):
- Postprandial abdominal pain (30–60 min after eating)
- Food fear (patient avoids eating)
- Weight loss
