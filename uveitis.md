Though the term uveitis means inflammation of the uveal tract (iris, ciliary body, and choroid), it can involve the adjacent structures like the retina, retinal vessels, vitreous, optic nerve head, and sclera.
## Anterior Uveitis
Inflammation of anterior part of uvea. i.e. Iris, ciliary body. 

Unilateral eye pain, photophobia and ciliary congestion. The pupil may be small and irregularly shaped due to spasm of the sphincter muscle in the iris.
- **Iritis**: inflammation limited to the iris. MC form of ant. uveitis. ciliary flush, photophobia, small irregular pupil, flare in anterior chamber
- **Iridocyclitis**: iris + ciliary body. Often used interchangeable with ant uveitis as ciliary body is usually always involved with iritis. 
- **Anterior cyclitis**: Rarely ciliary body inflamed alone. Usually occurs with iritis, hence iridocyclitis. 
## Intermediate uveitis
vitreous and peripheral retina (pars plana region)

- **Pars planitis**: used interchangeably with intermediate uveitis, but technically refers to the idiopathic form
- **Posterior cyclitis**: Not an independent term now. Merged under intermediate uveitis. 
- **Hyalitis**: Inflammation of the vitreous humour (Essentially, vitritis)- Not a type of uveitis itself but is present with intermediate uveitis
## Posterior uveitis
Inflammation involving: choroid, retina, sometimes optic nerve. 

- Choroiditis: Mainly choroid
- Chorioretinitis: choroid + retina. Very common form of posterior uveitis. Example: toxoplasma infection
- Retinochoroiditis: Same structures as chorioretinitis. Emphasis that retina is the primary site. Toxoplasma
- Retinitis: Retina. CMV retinitis in immunocompromised.
- Neuroretinitis: Inflammation of: optic nerve head + adjacent retina. Characteristic feature: optic disc swelling + macular star.

# Panuveitis
Entire uveal tract (anterior + intermediate + posterior).

Common exam-relevant causes include:
- [[behcet's disease|Behçet’s disease]]
- [[sarcoidosis]]
- Vogt–Koyanagi–Harada disease
- Sympathetic ophthalmia
- Endophthalmitis (severe intraocular infection)
