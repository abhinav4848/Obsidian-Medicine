Thromboangiitis obliterans (Buerger's disease & not [[glomerulonephritis|Berger's disease]]) is inflammation of small vessels of LL & occurs in young men who smoke. Presents with claudication and rest pain. Treatment is same as for PVD but patient must stop smoking.

- Compression therapy works by improving venous return, reducing oedema, and promoting wound healing.
- Intermittent pneumatic compression can be useful in patients who cannot tolerate or apply standard compression bandaging, It is generally reserved as a second-line treatment option and is less practical for daily use.

# ABPI

| ABPI          | Management                                                                     |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| < 0.5         | Life-threatening limb ischaemia - needs urgent vascular referral.              |
| 0.5 - 0.8     | Significant ischaemia (perhaps mixed vascular disease)                         |
| < 0.9         | arterial disease (avoid compression bandaging here)                            |
| ==0.9 - 1.2== | normal- Compression bandage okay for venous ulcer                              |
| > 1.3         | likely arteial disease/arteriosclerosis/arterial calcification (e.g. diabetes) |
