# Causes
- Viral: Rhinorrhoea, nasal congestion, and cough usually in viral, but not in bacterial pharyngitis.
- Influenza: Headache, weakness and fatigue, myalgia, malaise, anorexia, insomnia, a dry, unproductive cough, and fever
- Strep (GABHS)/strep pyogenes
	- Pharyngitis
	- [[tonsillitis]]
	- [[scarlet fever]]
- Adenovirus: Pharyngoconjunctival fever- fever and [[conjunctivitis]]. Malaise, myalgia, headache, rhinitis and cervical adenitis may also be present.
- [[cold sores|Herpes Simplex]]: HSV1 more than 2- Acute herpetic pharyngitis
- [[epstein barr virus|glandular fever]]- EBV
- Fusobacterium necrophorum- may cause pharyngitis/tonsillitis.
- Rare: Hib ([[acute epiglottitis]]), Enterovirus (herpangina & [[hand foot mouth|Hand, foot and mouth disease]]), [[measles]], Candida pharyngitis, [[gonorrhoea|Neisseria gonorrhoea]], [[diphtheria]], Chlamydophila & mycoplasma pneumonia. 
- Non-infectious: [[kawasaki disease]], drugs causing neutropenia/agranulocytosis/[[platelets|thrombocytopenia]], [[behcet's disease]] (oral & genital ulcer +/- uveitis), [[syphilis]] (single non-tender ulcer), [[aphthous ulcer]] (inside of lips)

# Red Flag
==People with a mouth ulcer that persists for more than 3 weeks should be referred urgently to a specialist.== #important
