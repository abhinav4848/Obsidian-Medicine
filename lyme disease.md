**Migrans**: migrating redness
# Clinical Features
Lyme disease
- Fever, arthralgia, heart block

**Target lesion**
- annular rash with scaly edges develops 3-30 days after h/o camping, walking in jungles.
- Lesion is due to an actual infection at the site. This is a single rash that expands. 
- ![[erythema chronicum migrans.jpg|300]]

Other rashes in Lyme
- Acrodermatitis chronica atrophicans (this is due toÂ _B. afzelii_)
- Borrelia lymphocytosis
# Investigation
ELISA for Borrelia antibodies.
# Treatment
Oral Doxycycline (but if pregnant -> Amoxicillin)
- Disseminated disease: Ceftriaxone

>[!notice]
>Doxycycline is 1st line in Lyme & [[chlamydia|Chlamydia]] [[gynaecological infections#Cervicitis|cervicitis]].
>Ceftriaxone is also in Meningococcal [[meningitis]], [[gonorrhoea]].

no need for prophylactic antibiotics for Lyme disease in asymptomatic patients bitten by a tick. 

Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction is sometimes seen after initiating therapy: fever, rash, tachycardia after first dose of antibiotic (more commonly seen in syphilis, another spirochaetal disease)
