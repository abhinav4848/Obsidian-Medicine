# OHSS
Following ovulation induction, women can get OHSS- symptoms are: 
- Nausea/vomiting/diarrhoea/ascites/abdo pain. 

Risk greatest with Gonadotropins. 

OHSS can be life threatening. 
- Can lead to hypovolemic shock, acute renal failure, VTE or arterial thromboembolism. 
## Management of OHSS
Most women with mild or moderate OHSS can be managed as an out-patient but should be managed by a team with specialist knowledge of OHSS.

Hospital admission should be considered for women who are unable to achieve satisfactory pain control, are unable to maintain adequate fluid intake due to nausea, show signs of worsening OHSS despite outpatient intervention, are unable to attend for regular outpatient follow-up or have critical OHSS.

All fertility centres should provide women undergoing fertility treatment with a contact number for advice and assessment should they develop any possible symptoms of OHSS.

# Normal Ovulation #notAKT 
Normal Ovulation: Involves GnRH, FSH, LH, and estradiol feedback loops.  

Categories of Anovulation:  
1. Class 1: Hypogonadotropic hypogonadal anovulation (e.g., hypothalamic amenorrhoea).  
2. Class 2: Normogonadotropic normoestrogenic anovulation (e.g., PCOS).  
3. Class 3: Hypergonadotropic hypoestrogenic anovulation (e.g., premature ovarian insufficiency).

### Management of anovulation
Ovulation induction may be performed with a number of different medications. 
#### 1. SERMs
Clomiphene citrate, [[cancer- drugs involved in gynae cancers|Tamoxifen]]
**Mechanism:**
- They block oestrogen receptors in the hypothalamus.
- The hypothalamus thinks oestrogen levels are low.
- This increases GnRH → FSH and LH release → follicular development → ovulation.

Clomiphene is the classic first-line drug for ovulation induction (though letrozole is now preferred in PCOS in many guidelines).
Raloxifene is not a medication used to induce ovulation.

#### 2. Aromatase inhibitors
Letrozole, Anastrozole
**Mechanism**:
- Low oestrogen -> increased FSH
- These are typically used after oral agents fail or in specialist fertility settings.

First-line medical therapy for patients with PCOS, due to the reduced risk of adverse effects on endometrial and cervical mucous compared to clomiphene citrate. 

#### 3. Gonadotrophins
FSH ± LH injections
**Mechanism**:
- Direct stimulation of the ovaries.

These are typically used after oral agents fail or in specialist fertility settings.
Gonadotrophin therapy is associated with an increased risk of ovarian hyper stimulation syndrome compared to clomiphene citrate, letrozole or anastrozole.
