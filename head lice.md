If live head louse is found in a family member, advise detection combing for all contacts. 
# Investigation
Detection combing is done on wet or dry hair with a special detection comb. 
# Management
- Only initiate treatment if live louse is found. Prophylactic treatment is not recommended. 
- Household contacts of patients with head lice do not need to be treated unless they are also affected
- Wet combing is a method of treatment for confirmed head lice infestation, involving systematically combing the hair over a 2-week period to remove lice.
- Malathion, wet combing, dimeticone, isopropyl myristate and cyclomethicone
	- Dimeticone is a physical insecticide and an effective treatment for head lice.
- School exclusion is not advised for children with head lice

Don't use Benzoyl Benzoate as a first-line treatment, due to its irritant nature and less effective results compared to other treatments.
