Stones/sludge impact cystic duct. But doesn't sit there- falls back into the gall bladder. That's why it's intermittent pain. 

# Clinical Features
[[abdominal pain|RUQ pain]] is sporadic and unpredictable, subsides gradually, lasting 30-90 mins.
Begins abruptly post-prandial (usually after a fatty meal). 
Intermittent, intense, dull, radiating to back and interscapular region. 
  
Approx. 20% of biliary colic patients go on to develop [[acute cholecystitis]].

# Epidemiology
**5Fs**: Fat, forty, fair, fertile, female. #mnemonic 


Pain in the RUQ radiating to the back and interscapular region, may be following a fatty meal. Slight misnomer as the pain may persist for hours
