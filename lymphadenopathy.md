[[cancer- lymphoma|HL]]: painless cervical lymphadenopathy. Involves more localised, central nodes (cervical/supraclavicular) > axillary > inguinal

[[leukaemia|CLL]]: generalised rather than localised lymphadenopathy, marked lymphocytosis on blood counts

[[epstein barr virus|EBV]] may cause cervical lymphadenopathy and sometimes pruritus, but it more commonly presents acutely with sore throat, fever, malaise and splenomegaly; Labs: atypical lymphocytosis rather than eosinophilia or anaemia.

Tuberculous lymphadenitis: Chronic cervical nodes but usually causes constitutional symptoms like prolonged fever and weight loss; nodes are often matted or fluctuant over time. Eosinophilia is not typical.

[[Sarcoidosis]] can cause non-tender lymphadenopathy and sometimes skin changes causing itchiness, but it more frequently involves bilateral hilar nodes on chest imaging rather than isolated cervical nodes; bloods may show hypercalcaemia or raised ACE levels instead of eosinophilia.
