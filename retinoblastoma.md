Retinoblastoma is the most common ocular malignancy found in children. The average age of diagnosis is 18 months.
Autosomal dominant. 
Associated with [[cancer- bone|osteosarcoma]] as same gene. 

# Clinical Features
Causes loss of red-reflex in young child (replaced by white pupil- leukocoria
- Red reflex could be lost due to congenital cataract but that would be detected at birth, or during routine baby-checks. 
Strabismus / esotropic strabismus.
- Esotropia is a type of strabismus where one or both eyes turn inward toward the nose, often giving a "cross-eyed" appearance
- See [NICE- Squint in children](https://cks.nice.org.uk/topics/squint-in-children/)
Visual problems. 
# Management
Refer 2ww to ophthal for absent red reflex.

Enucleation isn't the only option. 
Depending on how advanced the tumour is, other options include external beam radiation therapy, chemo, photocoagulation. 

Prognosis: 90% surviving into adulthood.
