**Definition**: Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is a heterogeneous lung condition characterized by chronic respiratory symptoms (dyspnea, cough, sputum production and/or exacerbations) due to abnormalities of the airways (bronchitis, bronchiolitis) and/or alveoli (emphysema) that cause persistent, often progressive, airflow obstruction. 
# Diagnosis of COPD
[NICE](https://cks.nice.org.uk/topics/chronic-obstructive-pulmonary-disease/diagnosis/diagnosis-copd/): Suspect COPD in patients > 35yo who have a risk factor (generally a [[smoking]] history of greater than 20 pack years, occupational, environmental exposure) and who present with one or more of the following symptoms:
 
1. Exertional breathlessness ([[mrc dyspnoea scale|MRC scale]])
2. Chronic cough
3. Regular sputum production >3 months.
4. Frequent winter 'bronchitis' / LRTI
5. Wheeze 
## vs Asthma
Night time waking with breathlessness and/or wheeze is more [[asthma]].
Significant diurnal or day-to-day variability of symptoms is more asthma.
Symptoms in age <35 are more common in asthma.

---
## Imaging
- Hyperinflated lung
- More than 7 posterior ribs seen (Hyperinflated lung)
- Flat diaphragm
- Small heart
- May see bullae
## Spirometry Criteria
You must confirm COPD by [[spirometry#COPD linked|spirometry]]. 
![[spirometry#COPD linked]]

If couldn't diagnose, do 'treat to diagnose' method with 6 week course of ICS (or few days of Oral steroid)-> If symptoms (Wheeze, SOB, cough) improve-> Asthma.
