# Anti Folate drugs
🚩The concurrent use of methotrexate and trimethoprim containing antibiotics may cause bone marrow suppression and severe / fatal pancytopenia. 
- [[trimethoprim]]
- [[methotrexate]] ([BNF Interactions- MTX](https://bnf.nice.org.uk/interactions/methotrexate/))
- [[malaria|proguanil]] / brand Malarone (combo of Atovaquone & Proguanil)- both are anti-malarial drugs. Avoid in pregnancy but can consider in T2/T3. 
# When to give
From before conception until 12 weeks PoG in females of childbearing potential
- Low risk female: 400mcg OD
	- Obesity is low risk, so they only need 400mcg.[^1] 
- High risk female: 5mg OD ([[diabetes- gestational|GDM]])
	- Either partner has NTD, previous pregnancy having NTD, f/h/o NTD
	- Woman taking antiepileptic drugs / [[coeliac disease]] / diabetes / thalassemia trait. 
- If sickle cell disease- They may already be on 5mg OD. Continue or increase to 5mg for full pregnancy. 

If getting MTX, s/e: 5mg once weekly, taken on a different day than MTX

Also advise it in couples trying to conceive ([[fertility]])



[^1]: From CKS: Reassure anyone with a body mass index (BMI) of 25 kg/m2 or more, or who has an increased risk of pre-eclampsia that they do not need to take more than 400 micrograms of folic acid a day unless they have an increased risk of having a baby with a neural tube defect or other congenital malformation.
