# Erythema Infectiosum 
- Fever, runny nose and headache, followed by slapped cheek appearance rash, spreads to body. 
	- Slapped cheek appearance fades over 1-4 days. 
- Itchy if soles of feet involved.  
- Spares nasolabial folds & eyes. 
## Treatment
Rest and analgesia
Once rash appears, child becomes non infectious so can return to [[School in children with rash|school]].

>[!warning]
>Aplastic crisis if h/o Sickle cell

## Complications
Can cause aplastic crisis if h/o [[Sickle cell]], thalassemia, spherocytosis-> Do urgent FBC & retic count if child breathless, dizzy, confused, lethargic (unwell child).

Pregnancy: If exposed in first 20 weeks, can cause fetal hydrops (at least 2 sites of edema in fetus) -> fluid overload can lead to fetal heart failure.
