The BNF issues guidance on good practice when prescribing, selected points include:  
- drugs should generally be prescribed by their generic name, except for certain preparations where the clinical effect may differ - See list below
- when writing numbers, unnecessary decimal points should be avoided e.g. 250 ml not 0.25 l
- it is a legal requirement for ==children under the age of 12 that their age is specified== on the prescription

# Drugs which should be prescribed by brand
Different mesalazine preparations have different release characteristics and are not bioequivalent. This means that different brands of mesalazine release the active drug at different rates and in different parts of the gastrointestinal tract, which can affect both efficacy and side effect profiles. Switching between different mesalazine preparations without clinical supervision could lead to suboptimal treatment or increased adverse effects

- modified release [[calcium channel blockers|CCB]]s
- antiepileptics
- [[ciclosporin]] and tacrolimus
- mesalazine (see [[inflammatory bowel disease|IBD]])
- [[lithium]]
- aminophylline and theophylline
- methylphenidate (see [[attention deficit hyperactive disorder|ADHD]])
- CFC-free formulations of beclometasone
- dry powder inhaler devices
