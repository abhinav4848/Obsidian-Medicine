Anal fissures are longitudinal or elliptical tears of the squamous lining of the distal anal canal. 
Acute: < 6 weeks 
Chronic: > 6 weeks.

Anal fissure can cause fresh rectal bleed when patient passes stool. 
# Inv
Don't do DRE as painful. 
Examination shows a longitudinal tear occurring posteriorly and in the midline.

The presence of a lateral anal fissure is unusual and requires further investigation. e.g. [[inflammatory bowel disease|Crohn's disease]] 
If patient has anal ulceration -> suspicious of anal cancer and therefore requires a 2-week wait referral to colorectal surgeons.
# Management
Acute < 1 week:
- Fibre & high fluid
- bulk forming laxative. If not tolerated, then lactulose
- Lubricants like petroleum jelly before defecation
- topical anaesthetic
Chronic
- Primary anal fissure> 1 week w/o concerning features, GTN ointment is a suitable analgesic.  
- if topical GTN is not effective after 8 weeks then secondary care referral should be considered for surgery (sphincterotomy)
    - lateral internal sphincterotomy is the gold standard for chronic anal fissure not responding to medical therapy
    - if sphincterotomy fails or is contraindicated then botulinum toxin injection is sometimes used
