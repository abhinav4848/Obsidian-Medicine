 Malcolm Knowles introduced the term 'andragogy' to a wider audience, defining it as “the art and science of helping adults learn.” Andragogy is based on assumptions about how adults learn and their attitude towards and motivation for learning. Knowles later also derived principles of andragogy which are used as guidelines on how to teach learners who tend to be at least somewhat independent and self directed.
# Principles of adult learning theory include:
- The learner should be an active contributor to the educational content and process
- Learning should closely relate to understanding and solving real life problems
- Learners current knowledge and previous experience are critical in new learning situations and need to be considered and built upon  
- Learners should be given the opportunity and support to use self direction in their learning
- Learners should be given opportunities and support for practice, along with self assessment and constructive feedback from teachers and peers
- Learners should be given opportunities to reflect on their practice: this involves analysing and assessing their own performance and developing new perspectives and options
- Use of role models by medical educators has a major impact on learners
