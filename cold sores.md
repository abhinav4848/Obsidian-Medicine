Primary attacks of Type 1 [[human herpes viruses|HSV]] infections occur mainly in infants and young children. In crowded, underdeveloped areas of the world, nearly all children have been infected by the age of 5. 

Oral Herpes simplex could be of two types: First presentation or Recurrent
# First presentation
### Herpes Gingivostomatitis
- Most common primary HSV1 presentation. More common in Children <5yo
- Diffuse oral involvement including the gingiva
### Acute herpetic pharyngitis
- More common in adolescents / adults. 
- Posterior throat involvement instead of gingiva
- Suggested by vesicles and shallow ulcers on the palate (although these are not always present). There may be cervical lymphadenopathy, fever, and exudate. If there is an associated gingivostomatitis, vesicles and ulcers are also present on the labial and buccal mucosa. 
- *See [[sore throat]].* 
# Recurrent infection
Herpes simplex labialis (cold sores)- happens at vermillion border of lips. 
1. Cold sores should start to heal within 10 days, but are contagious and may be irritating or painful while they heal. 
2. No restriction for [[school in children with rash|school]].
