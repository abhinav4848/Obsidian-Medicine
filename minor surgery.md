# Local anaesthetic (LA)
Lidocaine is the most widely used LA. It has a rapid onset of action and anaesthesia lasts for around 1 hour.  
- the maximum safe dose is 3mg/kg. 
	- BNF states 200mg (or 500mg if given in solutions containing adrenaline), for a 66kg patient, which equates to 3mg/kg. 
- This is the equivalent of 20ml of 1% solution or 10ml of 2% solution. 
	- (Think: 1% = 1 g per 100 mL → 1000 mg / 100 mL = 10 mg/mL)
- lidocaine is available pre-mixed with adrenaline. This increases the duration of action of lidocaine + reduces blood loss thanks to vasoconstriction. It must never be used near extremities of the body due to the risk of ischaemia
