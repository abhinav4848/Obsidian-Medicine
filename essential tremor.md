Essential tremor (previously called benign essential tremor) is an ==autosomal dominant== condition which usually affects both upper limbs. 
# Features 
- b/l postural tremor of 4-12 Hz (commonly 6-8 Hz): worse if arms outstretched
- improved by alcohol and rest
- most common cause of titubation (head tremor)
# Management  
- ==propranolol== is first-line
- primidone (barbiturate anticonvulsant) - useful alternative or adjunct if propranolol contraindicated or inadequate
