2ry amenorrhea (cessation of menstruation for > 6 months after it has been established)

This may prevent the endometrium responding to oestrogen as it normally would.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |

# Question
A 23-year-old woman presents for review. She has not had a normal period for around 8 months now. A recent pregnancy test was negative. Blood tests are ordered: 

|                             |                            |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| FSH                         | 2.2 IU/L (0-20 IU/L)       |
| Oestradiol                  | 84 pmol/l (100-500 pmol/l) |
| Thyroid stimulating hormone | 3.1 mIU/L                  |
| Prolactin                   | 2 ng/ml (0-10 ng/ml)       |
| Free androgen index         | 3 ( < 7 )                  |
What is the most likely cause of her symptoms?
A: Functional hypothalamic amenorrhoea. 
Not PCOS -> Raised androgen
Not Premature ovarian insufficiency -> High FSH & Low oestradiol
Not Hyperprolactinemia
