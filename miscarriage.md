Miscarriage occurs in approximately 25% of all recognised pregnancies. The majority of sporadic losses before 10 weeks' gestation result from random numeric chromosomal errors, specifically: trisomy (Down, Edward, Patau), monosomy and polyploidy. Women over the age of 35 years have a markedly increased risk of trisomy pregnancies. 

For women with a miscarriage diagnosed before 13 weeks' gestation, expectant management for 7-14 days is the first-line option, in the absence of factors such as evidence of infection, or increased risk of bleeding.
