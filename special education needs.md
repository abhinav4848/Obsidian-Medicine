A child has a significant learning disability and their parents are asking about special education needs (SEN) schooling. They get an Education, Health and Care plan (formerly ‘statement’). 

- SEN support is based on need, not diagnosis- So diagnosing a child as LD doesn't automatically entitles them to SEN
- It covers a child up to the age of 25 at further education colleges, but not universities
- EHC plans are legally binding on education and health services, so it's not advisory only
- If required, support is funded by the **local authority**, not parents
- Placement is based on **needs, not strict catchment rules**, so they don't need to live in the catchment of a SEN school.
