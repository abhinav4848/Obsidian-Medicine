**Triangulation** refers to the use of more than one method or approach to evaluate a process, individual or organisation. An example would be the use of multiple assessments (AKT, CSA, COTs, CEXs, patient feedback etc) when assessing a GP trainee. This decreases the likelihood that inaccurate results or outcomes are obtained due to one flawed assessment process.s, you can gather more accurate and reliable information about the trainee's performance.

**Theoretical sampling** is a technique used in qualitative research, particularly grounded theory, where researchers collect data based on emerging theoretical concepts. The aim is to develop a theory that explains a particular phenomenon or process. 
  
Finally, **Grounded theory** is a qualitative research methodology that aims to generate theories by systematically gathering and analysing data from multiple sources. Although both grounded theory and triangulation involve collecting data from various sources, grounded theory specifically focuses on generating theories through this process rather than assessing individuals' performance.
