**PMS**: Personal Medical Services Agreement

# QoF
The Quality and Outcomes Framework (QOF) is a voluntary annual reward and incentive programme for all GP practices in England, detailing practice achievement results. (Now doesn't exist in Scotland). It was introduced as part of the new General Medical Services (GMS) to incentivise not only the management of chronic disease such as diabetes but also to improve the organisation of the practice and patient experience. 
 
 It was introduced as part of the GMS contract in 2004 but is available to PMS practices as well. PMS practices can choose to participate in QOF to receive additional funding based on their performance against a set of indicators across clinical domains, public health domains, and quality improvement domains.

Majority of GP practices in the UK operate under the GMS contract. According to NHS Digital data, approximately 70% of practices use the GMS contract, while around 30% use the PMS contract. The proportion has shifted over time as some PMS practices have reverted to GMS contracts following funding reviews.

Other points  
- for clinical indicators the value of a point is determined by the prevalence of that condition in the practice
- participation in the QOF is voluntary
- 5% of practices should be visited at random to help prevent fraud.

The table below shows the three key areas on which the QOF is based  

|**Domain**|**Notes**|
|---|---|
|**Clinical indicators**|Standards linked to the care of patients suffering from chronic diseases. The largest domain of QOF|
|**Public health**|Smoking cessation, cervical screening, child health surveillance etc. The second largest domain of QOF|
|**Public health including additional services sub domain**|This sub domain has indicators across the two service areas of cervical screening and contraceptive services|
## Patients may be 'exception reported' if
- patients refused attendance even after 3 invites in last 12 months
- Inappropriate to review chronic disease parameters due to particular circumstances e.g. Terminal illness, extreme frailty
- patients newly diagnosed within the practice or who have recently registered with the practice, who should have measurements made within 3 months and delivery of clinical standards within 9 months e.g. Blood pressure or cholesterol measurements within target levels
- Patients on maximum tolerated dose but still sub-optimally controlled
- patients for whom prescribing a medication is not clinically appropriate e.g. Those who have an allergy, another contraindication or have experienced an adverse reaction
- where a patient has not tolerated medication
- where a patient does not agree to investigation or treatment (*informed dissent*), and this has been recorded in their medical records 
- where the patient has a supervening condition which makes treatment of their condition inappropriate e.g. Cholesterol reduction where the patient has liver disease
- where an investigative service or secondary care service is unavailable

Patients who are predominantly managed in secondary care should still be included in the Quality and Outcomes Framework (QOF) register and monitored in primary car

# GP contract: comparison
The table below summarises some of the basic differences the General Medical Services (GMS), Personal Medical Services (PMS) and Alternative Provider Medical Services contracts:  

|                               | **GMS**                                                                                                                | **PMS**                                                                         | **APMS**                                            |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **Who can provide services?** | GP / qualifying health professional or company 100% owned by qualifying persons                                        | GP / qualifying health professional or company 100% owned by qualifying persons | No restriction of providers                         |
| **Contract - negotiation**    | Nationally negotiated                                                                                                  | Locally negotiated with mandatory terms                                         | Locally negotiated with mandatory terms             |
| **Contract - scope**          | Essential services (e.g. day-to-day care) with optional additional services (e.g. Minor surgery) and enhanced services | Core and additional services (similar to GMS)                                   | Depends on individual contract                      |
| **Contract - type**           | Open-ended which cannot be terminated unless fault proven                                                              | Usually for five years, contract may be terminated                              | Usually for three years, contract may be terminated |
| **Payment**                   | Global sum, QOF, enhanced services, premises, IT                                                                       | Baseline set nationally, QOF                                                    | Locally negotiated                                  |
| **General**                   | The majority of practices are GMS                                                                                      | GPs in PMS tended to have higher incomes but not anymore after equalisation     |                                                     |
