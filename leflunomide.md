*(don't confuse with Lamotrigine- antiepileptic)*

Leflunomide is a [[rheumatoid arthritis|DMARD]] mainly used in the management of rheumatoid arthritis. Its active form, Teriflunomide has a very long half-life due to enterohepatic circulation. This should be remembered as it's highly teratogenic. 
# Contraindications
- pregnancy - BNF advises: '_Effective contraception essential during treatment and for at least 2 years after treatment in women and at least 3 months after treatment in men (plasma concentration monitoring required)_'
- caution should also be exercised with pre-existing lung and liver disease
# Adverse effects
- gastrointestinal, especially diarrhoea
- [[hypotension & hypertension drugs|Hypertension]]
- weight loss/anorexia
- peripheral neuropathy
- myelosuppression
- [[interstitial lung disease vs pneumonitis|pneumonitis]]
# Monitoring
See: [[monitoring bloods with drugs]] 
- FBC/LFT and blood pressure
# Stopping
- leflunomide has a very long wash-out period of up to a year which requires co-administration of cholestyramine (as it's enterohepatically circulated)
