Clinical features of Pre-eclampsia

Symptoms

- Frontal headache
- Visual disturbance (flashing lights and blurring)
- Epigastric or RUQ pain- more commonly associated with HELLP
- Nausea/vomiting
- Rapid edema: especially face, and, feet

Signs

- BP > 140/90 or > 160/110 severe
- Proteinuria > 300mg in 24 hrs/dipstick ++ to +++
- Facial edema
- papilloedema
- Epigastric/RUQ tenderness due to liver involvement and capsule distension
- Confusion or neurological disturbance
- Hyperreflexia or clonus: Signs of cerebral irritability
- Uterine tendernes or vaginal bleeding from placental abruption
- Fetal growth restriction (SGA <10th centile) or IUGR on USS
- platelet count < 100 * 10^6/l, abnormal liver enzymes or HELLP syndrome

- Can lead to hemorrhage: placental abruption, intra-abdominal, intra-cerebral

Labetalol is 1st line for pre-eclampsia and gestational HTN (Avoid if asthma. It's 3rd gen cardioselective with vasodilatory properties).

If not effective or woman has good control with nifedipine from before, to continue with nifedipine throughout the pregnancy. BP should be monitored regularly during pregnancy as it may reduce during pregnancy in which case the meds may be withheld until after 20 weeks or delivery, when BP starts to rise again.

Prevention

NICE divide risk factors into high and moderate risk:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|High risk factors|Moderate risk factors|
|- hypertensive disease in a previous pregnancy
- chronic kidney disease
- autoimmune disease, such as SLE, APLA
- type 1 or type 2 diabetes
- chronic hypertension|- first pregnancy
- age 40 years or older
- pregnancy interval of more than 10 years
- BMI of 35 kg/m² or more at first visit
- family history of pre-eclampsia
- multiple pregnancy|

Aspirin

- women with the following should take aspirin 75-150mg daily from 12 weeks gestation until the birth

- ≥ 1 high risk factors
- ≥ 2 moderate factors

- Aspirin is c/I in breastfeeding.
- Women with APLA take Aspirin + LMWH.

Methyldopa is pregnancy safe -> Women on methyldopa need an alternative drug within 2 days of delivery.
