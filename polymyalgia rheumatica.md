**PMR**: CK Normal, ESR > 30, CRP > 6
**[[Polymyositis]]**: Raised CK & ALP -> Could walk before, can't walk now.

![[polymyositis#Differences in conditions affecting muscles]]

---
# Clinical Features
Gradual onset, symmetrical symptoms. 
Morning ache & stiffness. 
- of proximal muscles (neck, shoulder, upper arm, pelvic girdle)
Muscles are tender, not weak.
- The weakness is due to myalgia (pain inhibition i.e. subjective weakness present but not objective weakness). 
- Pain improves on passive motion of joint. 

---
# Temporal arteritis
PMR is associated with Giant cell arteritis 
GCA rarely happens to people under 60 years old.
# Test
ESR
# Treatment
Responds dramatically to steroids.
- Give [[corticosteroid|Prednisolone]] 60mg/d with taper, 
	- Low dose aspirin, 
	- [[bisphosphonates]] to prevent [[osteoporosis]]. 
	- PPI for gastroprotection
- If no improvement in 1 week, increase steroid dose and re-review in another week. If still no improvement, consider other diagnosis.
