*It's a [[seronegative spondyloarthropathy]].* 

---
Arthritis developing after an infection elsewhere 
More common in young with [[hla-b27]]+ve.
# Clinical Features 
1. No fever.
2. Recent h/o infection- Develops within 3 weeks of initial infection and symptoms last around 4-6 months.
	- **GU**: [[chlamydia]], [[gonorrhoea]], [[UTI]] 
	- **Gastroenteritis**: Salmonella, campylobacter, Dysentery  
	- *Organism can't be retrieved from the joint.*
3. There may be maculopapular waxy rash ([[keratoderma blennorrhagica]] -> palm, sole), palpable cervical nodes. Small joints swollen.
4. Migratory oligoarthritis of lower limbs (knee, ankle) + back pain + extraarticular features.
	- [[dactylitis]] 

Aka **Reiter's Syndrome**: 
can't see, can't pee, can't climb a tree ([[Conjunctivitis]], Urethritis, [[arthritis]]) + Rash #mnemonic 
# Treatment
NSAID > Sulfasalazine, [[methotrexate]]
