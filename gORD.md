Retrosternal burning and regurgitation, worse on lying down or leaning forward and relieved by antacids.
# Indications for upper GI endoscopy:
- age > 55 years
- symptoms > 4 weeks or persistent symptoms despite treatment
- dysphagia
- relapsing symptoms
- weight loss

If endoscopy is negative, consider 24-hr oesophageal pH monitoring (the gold standard test for diagnosis)

Proton pump inhibitors should be stopped 2 weeks before an upper GI endoscopy. 

No need to stop Aspirin

# Laryngopharyngeal / Silent reflux (LPR)
Globus (feeling of a lump in throat), hoarseness and no red flags → ?laryngopharyngeal reflux.

Stomach acid & digestive enzymes flow back into larynx, giving feeling of lump in throat & hoarseness. Even though the acid came up, it may not affect oesophagus hence GERD at the same time as LRP is not necessary. 

# Globus hystericus
Globus hystericus is a common symptom of anxiety but is unlikely to present chronically without other autonomic symptoms associated with anxiety.
