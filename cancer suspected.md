# Children & Young People
Children and young people (0-24yrs): Refer for ==immediate== specialist assessment for leukaemia if: 
- unexplained petechiae  
- hepatosplenomegaly.

**Refer for specialist assessment within 48 hours** 
- Palpable abdominal mass: To investigate for possible neuroblastoma or Wilm's tumour. However, specific guidance exists for hepatosplenomegaly because of the risk of underlying leukaemia and immediate assessment is indicated.
- Fever/weight loss/Night sweats/pruritus/SoB + lymphadenopathy/splenomegaly
- New abnormality of cerebellar or CNS function
- Haematuria (visible & unexplained)
- USS/X-ray suggests sarcoma
- Unexplained lymphadenopathy

FBC in 48 hrs
- Bruising/Bleeding
- Pallor
- Persistent fatigue 
- Unexplained fever
- Unexplained persistent infections
- Generalised lymphadenopathy
- Persistent or unexplained bone pain
- Bone pain/swelling (unexplained) -> Xray in 48 hrs
- Lump increasing in size -> USS in 48 hrs
