# Folliculitis
A furuncle (also known as a boil) is a deeper, and more pronounced infection of the hair follicle.
Causes  
- Infectious:
    - **Bacterial**: Most commonly due to _Staphylococcus aureus_. ==Hot tub folliculitis== may also develop due to infection with _Pseudomonas aeruginosa_
    - **Fungal**: Candida species or dermatophytes, especially in immunocompromised individuals.
    - **Viral**: Herpes simplex virus can cause a folliculitis known as herpetic folliculitis.
- Non-infectious:
    - Chemical Folliculitis: Caused by topical steroids, oils, or tar.
    - Physical Folliculitis: Due to shaving, tight clothing, or occlusive dressings.
# Pseudo Folliculitis barbae
This condition predominantly affects men of black African or [[afro-caribbean|Afro Caribbean]] ancestry due to tightly curled hair, which tends to curve back into the skin after shaving, causing a foreign body reaction. 
Features:
- Chronicity, exacerbation by shaving, localisation to the beard area, and post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. 
- Persistent bumps and irritation in his beard area
- Multiple small, firm papules and occasional pustules on the cheeks and neck, with some areas of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation.
Recognising this pattern is important for appropriate management (modifying shaving technique or cessation).

# Folliculitis barbae
Refers specifically to bacterial infection (often Staphylococcus aureus) of hair follicles in the beard region. While it also causes papules and pustules, it typically presents with more acute inflammation, tenderness, possible pus formation, and sometimes systemic symptoms if severe. The chronic relapsing course linked to shaving favours pseudofolliculitis over infectious folliculitis.

![[folliculitis- pseudomonas.jpg|361]]
This image shows a widespread eruption of monomorphic erythematous papules and pustules, each centred around hair follicles, distributed symmetrically over the trunk and flanks. The lesions are discrete, uniform in size (2–5 mm), and many exhibit a central pinpoint pustule, consistent with bacterial folliculitis. The sudden onset, truncal distribution, and absence of comedones or systemic symptoms suggest Pseudomonas aeruginosa folliculitis, often acquired after exposure to contaminated water (e.g. hot tubs, whirlpools)

# Tinea barbae
A dermatophyte fungal infection affecting the beard area but usually presents with inflamed plaques or boggy swellings ([[kerion]]), sometimes with broken hairs or pustules; it is less common in healthy adults and would be unlikely without animal contact or occupational risk factors.
