Aka tibial apophysitis. 
# Epidemiology
Common in sporty male teens (10-15 years) during pubertal growth spurt. 
# Pathogenesis
Stress from extensor mechanism -> Inflammation of tibial tubercle where patellar tendon attaches -> visible swelling & pain on tibial tubercle.
*See [[limping child]].* 
# Clinical Features
Discomfort on activity but no pain on rest. 
- During running, jumping, coming down stairs. 
# Investigations
Diagnosis is clinical & routine radiographs are not necessary. 
# Management
During flare ups- PRICE, activity modification. 
Paeds physio. 
If no better, referral to ortho.
